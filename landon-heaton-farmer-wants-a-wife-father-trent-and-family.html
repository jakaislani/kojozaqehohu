    Landon Heaton Farmer Wants A Wife father Trent Edward Heaton passed away on November 17, 2022. Landon shared a tribute video for him.

    Heaton is a cattle rancher and farmer who enjoys bow hunting and training retrievers. Notably, he is one of four bachelor farmers featured in the American reality series Farmer Wants a Wife.

    The series first landed on The CW and aired from April 30 to June 25, 2008. It consisted of eight episodes, during which 10 women try to be chosen by a bachelor farmer.

    After 15 years of hiatus, the American version returned to Fox on Wednesday, March 8, 2023, with singer Jennifer Nettles serving as host.

    This time, the show follows four seasonal farmers and 32 women. It helps the men connect with a gaggle of unattached women who aren’t having any luck finding love in the big city.

    The men and women meet and mutually decide whether love, chemistry, and the future can be embraced, all while living in their farm settings.

    RyanBlack, Hunter Grayson, Heaton, and Allen Foster are the four farmers. The 32 women include Meghan Baker (who left the show in E6 due to a family emergency), Stephanie, and Emma.

    Landon From Farmer Wants A Wife Lost His Dad Last Year

    Landon Heaton father Trent Edward Heaton passed away last year on November 17, 2022. He was 65 years old.

    The farmer recently shared a tribute video in memory of his late dad on Instagram. “You can’t pick your dad, but if you could, I’d pick him every time,” the rancher captioned the post.

    He pinned Zac Brown Band’s song My Old Man to the video and said he was a father, an outdoorsman, a grandfather, and the husband he aspires to be.

    Born and raised in Alva, Trent studied at Alva High School and graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Accounting and Business from Northwestern Oklahoma State University in 1980.

    He then went to work in the banking and oil sector in Oklahoma City. Heaton married Melissa Rennie in 1980 and moved to Ulysses, Kansas to help with the family’s natural gas business.

    The Heatons returned to Alva in 1991, and he took over the family ranching and farming business.

    Heaton enjoyed spending fun time with his grandchildren on the farm, watching them compete in sports, listening to classic rock music and concerts, and fishing with his family.

    He loved football and was a lifelong fan of the Dallas Cowboys and Oklahoma State Cowboys.

    Trent is survived by his loving wife Melissa, his three kids, Lance, Lauren, and Landon, and his three grandkids, Avyn, Asbrie, and Hutchison Heaton.

    His mother is Melissa Heaton

    Landon Heaton mother Melissa was born in Ponca City, Oklahoma, to Charles and Jane Rennie. She grew up alongside her brothers Mark Andrew Rennie and Martin Rennie.

    Sadly, her brother, Mark, passed away on May 12, 2012, after a tragic fall. He was at OU/Presbyterian Medical Center when he breathed his last.

    Heaton married Trent in 1981 in her hometown of Ponca City, Oklahoma. Later, the family moved to Kansas to help with the Heaton family business. They moved to Alva in 1991.

    A mother of three, Melissa loves playing with her grandkids and is often seen at family gatherings and events.

    Landon Farmer Wants A Wife Family

    Landon Heaton from Farmer Wants A Wife family consisted of his parents and two siblings. He has a brother named Lance and a sister named Lauren.

    His brother is a married man

    His brother Lance is one of three children of Melissa and Trent. Growing up in Alva, he studied at Northwestern Oklahoma State University.

    He is married to Brenna Heaton and has three kids -Asbrie, Avyn, and Hutchison Heaton- with her. He is active on Facebook, sharing loving pictures of his three little nuggets.

    In December 2020, he and his family had an exciting trip to Cumberland Pass (elevation 12,034 feet), a high mountain pass that lies in the Sawatch Range of the Rocky Mountains of Colorado.

    He also shared a picture of himself with his kids in front of the Cumberland Pass sign.

    In September 2021, Heaton went for another thrilling adventure with his kids, this time in Tincup Pass, a historic pass offering great scenery on both sides of the Continental Divide.

    The high mountain pass, located on the boundary of Chaffee and Gunnison counties, is at an elevation of 12,171 feet above sea level.

    Lauren works at Circus Street

    Farmer Wants a Wife star Heaton’s little sister Lauren works at Circus Street as the head of marketing. She joined the agency in April 2019.

    Circus Street is a global leader in providing digital skills, including data analytics, e-commerce, and digital marketing skills. It partners with global businesses to help people learn and work smarter.

    In January 2020, she shared she would be starting her MBA while working full-time via FB. She thanked Miss Rodeo America Scholarship Foundation for offering flexible options to use the scholarships.

    “It’s been 5 years since my reign and I will be able to graduate without having to take out a student loan,” she concluded.

    Yes, Lauren was Miss Rodeo Oklahoma 2014. If you scroll down her FB page, you will find multiple congratulatory messages from friends, family, and close ones.

    Who Does Landon Pick On Farmer Wants A Wife?

    Landon picks Ashley R. and Ashley L. as his final two women in Episode 9 of Farmer Wants a Wife. The episode aired on May 3 on Fox.

    In the previous episode titled “Is There Room for One More?” farmers went on their final dates with the women, they chose at the end of the bard dance in week seven in Tallahassee, FL.

    In Oklahoma, Heaton takes Zoe on her first solo date. He spritzes himself with cologne and takes her to check the crops before the date. He then shows her some seeds before going to an ATV for lunch.

    She tells the rancher she hasn’t dated since her divorce. And although Heaton says she is gorgeous, they don’t seem to have any chemistry.

    Surprisingly, Meghan returns to Hunter’s homestead after her grandfather’s funeral. The farmer is thrilled but the other ladies don’t seem to be happy, after all, she was the front-runner.

    At the end of the eighth episode, Hunter and Heaton are left to make their eliminations to get to the final two women.

    Heaton had three hopefuls -Zoe, Ashley L., and Ashley R. He did tell Ashley R. that he was going to choose her as one of the people and going to meet his daughter. So a little bit of a decision was made in Episode 8.

    In Episode 9, the rancher asks Zoe Weaver to leave with a heavy heart. “Unfortunately, I don’t think we’re at the same point in life,” he said to her.
